Social Business: The Language of Peace in a Fractured World

The Social Business Day is an annual global gathering organised by the Yunus Centre, convening entrepreneurs, global leaders, academics, policymakers, innovators, and youth from around the world to share experiences and explore new pathways toward a sustainable and humane future.

LEARN MORE

Contribution of Social Business to a more peaceful, inclusive, and sustainable world

Today, the world stands at a difficult crossroads. Conflicts between nations are intensifying, inequality continues to widen, and climate disruption is reshaping economies and societies. In many places, trust in institutions is eroding, leaving communities searching for new models that place human well-being at the centre of progress.

The conversation at the 16th Social Business Day will explore how this idea can continue to grow and evolve. Through keynote addresses, plenary discussions, thematic sessions, and showcases of innovative initiatives, participants will exchange ideas on entrepreneurship, technology, education, finance, climate action, and youth leadership.

A Global Festival of Social Business, People, and Possibility

In 2026, Grameen will mark two historic and deeply connected milestones: 50 years of Grameen Bank, and 20 years since the Nobel Peace Prize was awarded to Professor Muhammad Yunus and Grameen Bank, recognizing the idea that poverty is not created by the poor, but by the systems that surround them.

A Festival, Not a Ceremony

Rather than a single commemorative event, the anniversary will be observed as a year‑long global festival. The festival will begin in Bangladesh, where the idea of microcredit first took shape in Jobra Village, and will formally open during Social Business Day 2026 in Dhaka. From there, the celebration will continue throughout the year, spreading across countries, cities, communities, and villages worldwide.
